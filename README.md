Background
The family of freshwater sunfish (Centrarchidae) is a family of fish native to North America. Four species of this family however are invasive to South Africa. These are the Bluegill, Smallmouth bass, Largemouth bass and Spotted bass. These fish were first introduced as gamefish for freshwater fishing for South Africa since most of the native species are to small to fulfill this role. In the present day these four species have thrived in South african waters and spread through a majority of our freshwater system. These fish have had destructive impacts on our native freshwater fish species causing the decline in many of them. These fish also remain a primary target for many freshwater fisherman and this is the main reason for my interest in plotting the occurance of this fish on a map of South Africa. My goal for this project therefore is to find out where each of these four fish species are distributed throughout South Africa. A little secondary objective was to use this map to discover potential new fishing areas for me to go fish. 

Data
The data for this project comes from the freshwater biodivesity information system fish data.
